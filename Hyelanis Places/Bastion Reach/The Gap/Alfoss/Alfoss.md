# Districts

## **Centennial Park:**  
The covens had planted fast-growing trees. Now there is an impenetrable green barrier. The park has also swallowed several city blocks and is about four times its original size. This is the home of the Witches’ Oracle.  
#### The Witches’ Oracle: 
A mysterious gathering place deep within the heart of the park, where witches and mystics commune with the spirits of nature and the arcane. It's said that the Oracle can reveal glimpses of the future to those who dare to seek its guidance.
#### Moonlit Pond: 
A serene pond surrounded by shimmering willow trees, said to be inhabited by ancient water spirits that offer wisdom to those who seek their counsel
#### Enchanted Grove Apothecary: 
A mystical shop nestled within the heart of the park, exchanging rare herbs, potions, and magical artifacts crafted from the natural elements.
#### Whispering Willows Tea House: 
A tranquil tea house located on the outskirts of the park, known for its soothing herbal blends and serene atmosphere.
   
## **Unicorn Lane:**
Every city has one of those neighborhoods — dangerous, sinister places — so treacherous that even the criminals who prey on other criminals shun them. Unicorn Lane is such a place. Thirty city blocks long and eight blocks deep, it cut through what used to be Midtown like a dagger. Rubble chokes the streets and sewage overflows from the busted pipes in foul-smelling streams.  
#### The Black Market: 
A shadowy marketplace where contraband goods, forbidden magic, and illicit services are traded under the cover of darkness.
#### The Labyrinth: 
A maze-like network of narrow streets and alleys that twist and turn unpredictably, making navigation difficult for outsiders and law enforcement.
#### Shadowcloak Outfitters: 
A clandestine shop selling enchanted cloaks, disguises, and magical trinkets favored by spies and rogues operating within the district.
#### The Temple: 
A place of worship bordered on three sides by brick buildings and by a brick fence on the fourth, it has bright red walls, a white colonnade, and white stairs. Religious script hangs along the walls and keeps Unicorn Lane at bay. The wall itself has angelic names, magic squares, and holy names written on it. Inside, enormous windows let in light. Pews are cream with dark red cushions. It has cream walls and a high, vaulted ceiling gilded with gold.
   
## **Honeycomb Gap:**
Tucked away on the outskirts is a retirement community with a dark secret lurking beneath its seemingly tranquil facade. Once a haven for the city's elderly population, Honeycomb Gap has become a perilous maze of twisted homes and shadowed alleyways.A park retirement community. Homes warped from a freak magic surge. A fifth of the inhabitants vanished into the walls into “Outside.” saturated with magic.  
#### Wonderland Curiosities: 
A quirky shop filled with oddities and trinkets from "Outside," offering glimpses into other worlds and dimensions through its eclectic collection of artifacts.
#### The Whispering Walls: 
A series of ancient murals adorning the walls of the retirement homes, said to whisper secrets and forgotten memories to those who listen closely.

## **Warrens:**
Large, sturdy apartment complexes and two to three story office buildings.Crumbling apartment complexes loom over narrow alleyways, their facades marred by layers of graffiti and peeling paint. Two to three-story office buildings stand as monuments to the industrious spirit of the district, their weathered exteriors bearing the scars of years of neglect and hard use.  
#### The Towering Archive: 
A towering library that houses ancient tomes, scrolls, and manuscripts, attracting scholars and historians seeking to uncover the city's secrets.
#### The Gilded Goblet Tavern: 
A popular drinking establishment known for its rowdy atmosphere and exotic concoctions. The tavern caters to adventurers, mercenaries, and magic-users alike, offering potent brews and enchanted elixirs rumored to grant temporary boosts to strength, agility, and intellect.
#### The Mole Hole: 
It used to be Molen Enterprises with a slender glass tower. It was owned by one of the richest families in Alfoss Rumor says they got hold of a phoenix egg and planned to have it imprint on them. But phoenix rise like rockets into the sky when born, so instead the explosion took out the tower and three city blocks. It left a round crater about 140 feet across and fifty feet deep. Two weeks later it cooled and left a sheet of foot-thick glass on the bottom. People cut steps into the sides and turned it into an amphitheater. It sits in the middle of the city.
   
## **Champion Heights:** 
The epitome of opulence and grandeur, perched atop a scenic hill overlooking the rest of Alfoss. This district is home to the city's elite and wealthy aristocrats, who reside in sprawling mansions and luxurious estates surrounded by meticulously landscaped gardens and ornate iron gates. The streets are wide and immaculately paved, lined with majestic oak trees and gas lamps that cast a warm glow at night. Lavish carriages and sleek caravans ferry the affluent denizens to and from their lavish abodes, while private guards patrol the streets to ensure the safety and security of the privileged few.  
#### The Golden Gala:
An exclusive social club where the city's elite gather to mingle, network, and revel in luxury. The Golden Gala hosts extravagant balls, soirées, and private events, with sumptuous feasts, live entertainment, and opulent décor that rival the courts of kings and queens.
#### The Crystal Cathedral:
A magnificent temple dedicated to the city's patron deity, adorned with sparkling crystal spires and stained glass windows depicting scenes from ancient myths and legends. The cathedral serves as a place of worship and reflection for the wealthy inhabitants of Champion Heights, who spare no expense in their devotion to the divine.
#### Bernard's:
A restaurant where the elite gather to see and be seen and violence is forbidden. The building is an over-sized, Enlightenment-style mansion of redbrick, huge windows, and dark ivy climbing the walls.
   
## **The Hive:** 
The Hive bustles with activity at all hours, earning its name from the constant hum of commerce that fills the air. Located at the heart of the city, this vibrant market district is a melting pot of cultures, where merchants from all corners of the realm converge to buy, sell, and trade their wares. The narrow cobblestone streets are lined with colorful stalls and bustling shops, each one vying for the attention of passersby with shouts of bargains and enticing displays.  
#### [[Mercenary Guild]] Hall
The building used to be an old hotel built as a hollow tower. There was a restaurant, a coffee shop, a gift shop, and a raised platform area for happy hour. There was a stream running through the area and a lift. Now, the happy hour platform holds the job board, the gift shop is an armory, the restaurant is the mess hall, the lift doesn’t work, and the stream is gone.
#### The Steel Horse
A speakeasy on the border between [[The People]]’s and [[The Pack]]’s territories. It caters to both factions as long as they keep it civil. Only the bouncers are allowed real weapons. The building is an ugly, squat, and brick, with steel bars over the windows and a metal door over two inches thick. a hidden oasis of revelry and intrigue. Accessed through a nondescript doorway tucked away in a shadowy alley, the speakeasy beckons to those seeking refuge from the harsh realities of life in Alfoss.
#### Hammer & Anvil Forge:
A blacksmith's shop renowned for crafting enchanted weapons and armor, where skilled artisans combine traditional craftsmanship with magical enchantments.
#### The Thieves' Den: 
Tucked away in the labyrinthine alleys of The Hive lies the infamous Thieves' Den, a shadowy enclave where illicit goods and stolen treasures change hands under the cover of darkness. Rumors abound of secret entrances and hidden passages leading to this clandestine marketplace, where only the most daring and resourceful dare to venture.
#### The Artisan's Quarter: 
A haven for craftsmen and artisans, the Artisan's Quarter is a maze of workshops and studios where skilled craftsmen hone their trades. Here, intricate tapestries are woven, fine pottery is sculpted, and delicate jewelry is crafted with painstaking precision. Visitors can watch master artisans at work or browse their exquisite creations in the quaint storefronts that line the cobblestone streets.
