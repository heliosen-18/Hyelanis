***The Bridged Cities***

# Location and Geography
- **Wakabron Bay:** The city is situated at the mouth of Wakabron Bay, where a small inlet creates a natural division within the city.
- **The Unification Bridge:** A massive bridge spans the inlet, serving as the central point of the city and housing The Diarchs' Hold.
- **Eastern and Western Sides:** Dwarves primarily occupy the eastern side of the inlet known as Taruum, while gnomes are settled on the western side known as Val.

# The Diarchs' Hold: The Capital Palace
Built atop the Unification Bridge, symbolizing the unity and shared rule of the dwarven and gnomish monarchs.  
- **Dwarven and Gnomish Wings:** Features distinct wings or sections dedicated to each culture, with their respective architectural styles and aesthetics. The Dwarven wing being on the east side and Gnomish on the west.
- **Central Council Chambers:** Located at the center of the bridge where the two sides meet, emphasizing cooperation and shared governance.

# City Structure
## The Unification Bridge
The Unification Bridge has multiple levels, housing the following levels:

- **The Grand Concourse:** The top level, serving as a transit hub for airships and potentially housing important government offices.
- **The High Ward:** Aristocracy housing level with luxurious homes and gardens.
- **The Merchant's Mile:** Bustling marketplace with a mix of dwarven and gnomish goods.
- **The Mid Ward:** Middle-class housing level with a mix of shops and residences.
- **The Maker's Row:** Workshop level where dwarven and gnomish artisans collaborate.
- **The Low Ward:** Lower-class housing level with more basic accommodations and workshops.

## Beyond the Bridge:
##### Val (Western Gnomish Districts):
- **Whisperwind Heights:** Homes built into hillsides and elegant towers, featuring wind chimes, luminous gardens, and the Stargazer's Eye.
- **Gearsmith Gardens:** A hub of gnomish innovation with workshops, studios, the Clockwork Heart, and the Gnomish Academy of Invention.
- **Crystalfall District:** Built around waterfalls, featuring hidden grottoes, the Crystalfall Market, and vibrant nightlife.
##### Tarum (Eastern Dwarven Districts):
- **The Carven City:** Carved into the mountainside, featuring statues, grand halls, The Forges of An'dul Khaz'al, and the Great Library of Taruum.
- **The Deepforge District:** The industrial heart of the city with massive forges, skilled smiths, and the Dwarven Mining Guild.
- **The Anvilmount Ward:** Built on terraces, featuring sturdy homes, artisan workshops, and the Dwarven Museum of History.

## Economy and Trade
##### Wakabron Bay and the Inlet: 
Val Taruum's strategic location at the mouth of Wakabron Bay, straddling a small ocean inlet, fuels its diverse economy and trade. The expansive bay and the more contained inlet provide unique opportunities for both dwarven and gnomish merchants.   
##### **Dwarven Bay Trade:** 
The dwarves, with their sturdy ships and seafaring prowess, dominate trade in Wakabron Bay. Their focus lies in:
- **Shipbuilding:** Constructing robust cargo vessels and fishing boats in the bustling shipyards along the eastern shores.
- **Fishing:** Utilizing the rich resources of the bay to provide a steady supply of fish for the city and surrounding regions.
- **Export of Metals and Stone:** Shipping the high-quality metals, gems, and stonework produced by dwarven artisans to distant ports, establishing trade routes across the empire.  
##### **Gnomish Inlet Trade:** 
The gnomes, with their innate curiosity and knack for innovation, have established a thriving trade within the ocean inlet. Their focus lies not on traditional seafaring, but on the creation of marvels that take to the skies and push the boundaries of magical engineering. Their expertise lies in:
- **Airship Construction:** Utilizing their mastery of engineering and enchanting, the gnomes construct magnificent airships that soar through the skies of [[Finehold]]. These vessels range from nimble, single-passenger skiffs to grand galleons capable of carrying large groups and cargo. The gnomes' innovative designs and intricate enchantments make their airships highly sought-after throughout the empire.
- **Magical Invention:** The gnomes' workshops along the inlet buzz with creative energy as they tinker and experiment with new forms of magical technology. Their inventions include:
	- _Clockwork Automatons:_ Self-moving constructs that perform various tasks, from assisting in workshops to serving as messengers or even entertainers.
	- _Alchemical Devices:_ Ingenious contraptions that harness the power of alchemy, creating wondrous effects like self-lighting lanterns, ever-burning torches, and even miniature weather-controlling devices.
	- _Enchanted Tools:_ Everyday tools imbued with magical properties, such as self-sharpening axes, hammers that never miss their mark, and saws that can cut through the hardest stone with ease.
- **Exploration and Cartography:** Driven by their insatiable thirst for knowledge and discovery, gnomish explorers take to the skies in their airships to chart the unknown reaches of [[Hyelanis]]. They meticulously map out treacherous mountain ranges, hidden valleys, and uncharted islands, braving dangerous territories like the World Break to expand the empire's understanding of the world. Their detailed maps, enhanced with magical annotations and three-dimensional projections, are highly prized by merchants, adventurers, and military strategists alike. Gnomes also offer their expertise in aerial navigation, guiding expeditions and ensuring safe passage through the skies.
##### **The Merchant's Mile:** 
The central marketplace on the Unification Bridge, known as The Merchant's Mile, serves as a bustling hub where dwarven and gnomish merchants converge. Here, the diverse products of both cultures are traded, creating a vibrant economic center for the entire city. The Merchant's Mile attracts traders from across the empire, seeking the unique and high-quality goods that Val Taruum offers.
 
## Culture and Society
##### Bridge Life: 
The Unification Bridge is a melting pot of cultures, where dwarves and gnomes interact daily, working, trading, and living side-by-side. This has created a unique blend of traditions and lifestyles, fostering understanding and cooperation between the two races.  
##### Religion:
- Dwarves: The dwarves primarily worship Purphoros, the Ever Smith, reflecting their focus on craftsmanship, stonework, and forging, as well as their reverence for tradition and ancestral heritage.  
- Gnomes: The gnomes hold Keranos, the Storm Bringer, as their patron deity, embodying their love of innovation, tinkering, airship technology, intellectual curiosity, and pursuit of knowledge.  
##### Festivals: 
Val Taruum hosts numerous festivals throughout the year, many of which celebrate the cooperation between the dwarves and gnomes. These festivals feature boat races, bridge-spanning feasts, performances, and the "Diarch's Day" celebration.
##### Airship Wonders: 
The gnomes' mastery of airship technology is a source of pride and wonder in Val Taruum, with airship races and aerial displays being popular forms of entertainment.
##### Water and Nature: 
The presence of Wakabron Bay and the ocean inlet has influenced cultural practices in Val Taruum, with water-based rituals and ceremonies being common. The gnomes have a particularly strong connection to water, incorporating it into their magic and technology.
##### Dwarven Traditions: 
The dwarves maintain their rich cultural heritage, with traditions focused on craftsmanship, stonework, and ancestral reverence. Storytelling is a valued practice, preserving dwarven history and values.
##### Gnomish Innovation: 
The gnomes' culture is characterized by their love of innovation, tinkering, and discovery. They value creativity, ingenuity, and the pursuit of knowledge, constantly seeking new ways to improve their inventions and explore the world around them.

## Defenses
Val Taruum benefits from a multi-layered defense system that combines natural barriers, dwarven fortifications, and gnomish ingenuity.  
##### Natural Barriers:  
- _Wakabron Bay and the Inlet:_ The city's location at the mouth of Wakabron Bay and the ocean inlet provides natural defenses against land-based attacks, limiting enemy approach routes.    
- _The Unification Bridge:_ The bridge itself serves as a chokepoint, easily defended by both dwarven and gnomish forces. Its height also offers strategic vantage points for archers and magic users.  
 
##### Dwarven Fortifications:  
- _Eastern Walls:_ Sturdy walls and fortifications line the eastern side of the city, protecting it from threats from the mountains or inland territories.  
- _The Deepforge District:_ The forges in this district can be quickly repurposed to produce weapons and armor in times of need.  
- _Dwarven Guardians:_ Highly skilled dwarven warriors and guards patrol the city, particularly the eastern districts and the Unification Bridge, maintaining constant vigilance.
 
##### Gnomish Ingenuity:  
- _Hidden Tunnels and Traps:_ A network of hidden tunnels and secret passages, known only to the gnomes, provides quick movement throughout the city and access to strategic defensive positions. These tunnels may also be rigged with ingenious traps to deter invaders.  
- _Illusions and Misdirection:_ Gnome magic users can create illusions to confuse and disorient enemies, making it difficult for them to navigate the city or locate key targets.  
- _Airship Patrols:_ Gnomish airships patrol the skies above Val Taruum, providing aerial surveillance and early warning of approaching threats. They can also serve as a rapid response force, deploying defenders to any area under attack.
