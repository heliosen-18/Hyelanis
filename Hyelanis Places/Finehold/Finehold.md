## **Major Cities**

- **[[Val Taruum NPCs]] (Capital)**
    - Brief Summary: A bustling metropolis built on a unique bridge that spans a narrow inlet, creating a city divided into two distinct halves. The eastern side is home to the industrious dwarves, while the western side is inhabited by the inventive gnomes.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city's central feature is the Unification Bridge, a massive structure that houses not only the capital palace but also a bustling marketplace, workshops, and residences.
- **Men Kuldir**
    - Brief Summary: A sprawling underground city carved into the heart of a mountain, renowned for its skilled miners and the exceptional quality of its metalwork and gemstones. It is also a major trade hub for the entire empire.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is said to be connected to a network of tunnels that span the entire mountain range, allowing the dwarves to travel vast distances without ever seeing the light of day.
- **Kalgrum**
    - Brief Summary: A subterranean city built within a massive cavern, renowned for its impressive architecture and the intricate carvings that adorn its walls and ceilings.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is illuminated by bioluminescent fungi that grow in abundance throughout the cavern, creating a mesmerizing display of natural light.
- **Kulgrum**
    - Brief Summary: A fortress city built into the side of a dormant volcano, known for its skilled warriors and its impressive defenses.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city's forges are powered by the residual heat of the volcano, allowing the dwarves to craft weapons and armor of exceptional strength and durability.
- **Wheelwallow**
    - Brief Summary: A whimsical city built on a series of interconnected platforms and bridges, known for its eccentric inventors and its love of all things that spin, twirl, and rotate.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is powered by a massive water wheel that drives a network of gears and pulleys, providing energy for homes, workshops, and even a series of rotating platforms that serve as public transportation.
- **Cooperglen**
    - Brief Summary: A peaceful city nestled in a secluded valley, renowned for its skilled artisans and the beautiful handcrafted goods they produce.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is home to a unique species of tree that produces wood that is naturally infused with magic, allowing the gnomes to craft items with wondrous properties.
- **Skipperdale**
    - Brief Summary: A lively port city built on the shores of a vast lake, known for its skilled sailors and its thriving trade with other lakeside communities.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is home to a school of aquatic gnomes who have adapted to life underwater, building their homes and workshops beneath the surface of the lake.
- **Glimhollow**
    - Brief Summary: A hidden city built within a vast network of caves, known for its secretive inhabitants and its connection to the Underdark.
    - Interesting Quirk: The city is said to be guarded by a colony of giant bats that patrol the surrounding caves, protecting the gnomes from unwanted visitors.

## **Minor Cities and Towns**

- **Dunstone**
    - Brief Summary: A small mining town built into the side of a cliff, known for its hardy inhabitants and its production of high-quality iron ore.
    - Interesting Quirk: The town is home to a unique breed of mountain goat that is said to be able to sniff out valuable ores, aiding the miners in their search for new veins.
- **Zharr-Naggrund**
    - Brief Summary: A remote dwarven outpost built into the side of a mountain pass, serving as a guard post and a trading hub for travelers crossing the treacherous peaks.
    - Interesting Quirk: The outpost is built around a natural hot spring, providing the dwarves with a constant source of warmth and a place to relax after a long day's work.
- **Karak-a-Thul**
    - Brief Summary: A fortified dwarven settlement built on a small island in the middle of a Wakabron Bay, known for its skilled shipbuilders and its impressive naval fleet.
    - Interesting Quirk: The settlement is protected by a series of underwater tunnels and hidden traps, making it nearly impossible for enemies to attack by surprise.
- **Moonhaven**
    - Brief Summary: A secluded gnomish village built in a clearing deep within a forest, known for its peaceful inhabitants and its connection to the natural world.
    - Interesting Quirk: The village is said to be invisible to those who do not believe in the power of magic, making it a safe haven for those who are persecuted for their magical abilities.
- **Evergleam**
    - Brief Summary: A vibrant gnomish town built on a series of treetop platforms, known for its colorful inhabitants and its beautiful glowing plants and flowers.
    - Interesting Quirk: The town is said to be powered by a network of enchanted crystals that harness the light of the moon and stars, creating a dazzling display of light at night.
- **Gizgrotto**
    - Brief Summary: A quirky gnomish town built into the side of a cliff, known for its eccentric inventors and its strange and wondrous contraptions.
    - Interesting Quirk: The town is home to a giant, perpetually-spinning gear that powers all of its workshops and homes, creating a constant humming sound throughout the town.
- **Ocksdell**
    - Brief Summary: A peaceful gnomish village built in a secluded grove, known for its harmonious way of life and its deep connection to nature.
    - Interesting Quirk: The village is said to be protected by a group of sentient trees that can communicate with the gnomes and defend them from harm.
- **Sprigfiddel**
    - Brief Summary: A lively gnomish town built on a series of floating platforms on a large lake, known for its festive atmosphere and its love of music and dance.
    - Interesting Quirk: The town is said to be home to a magical spring that produces water that can enhance musical abilities, making it a popular destination for bards and musicians.
